Sarah Battersby - Research

As a researcher, my area of emphasis is primarily in Geographic Information Science (GIScience), with specialization in cognitive cartography and visualization. My research emphasizes theories and tools for visualizing information, cognitive research in the role of visualization in problem solving, and map projections. I also have strong interest in development of critical spatial thinking abilities through formal and informal geospatial education.

Working with Map Projections: A Guide to their Selection
Fritz Kessler and Sarah Battersby; 2019 CRC Press
A map projection fundamentally impacts the mapmaking process. Working with Map Projections: A Guide to Their Selection explains why, for any given map, there isnâ€™t a single "best" map projection. Selecting a projection is a matter of understanding the compromises and consequences of showing a 3-D space in two dimensions. The book presents a clear understanding of the processes necessary to make logical decisions on selecting an appropriate map projection for a given data set. The authors discuss the logic needed in the selection process, describe why certain decisions should be made, and explain the consequences of any inappropriate decision made during the selection process.
